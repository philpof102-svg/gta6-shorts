The GTA 6 Shorts pipeline

GTA 6 drops November 19. The faceless channels that win launch week are being built right now, not the week before. Here is the exact pipeline: one command turns a trailer into ten ready-to-shoot Shorts, plus the honest math on what it can and cannot do.

~6 min readopen-sourcebookmark it before it scrolls away

Every hype window follows the same script. A big launch is coming, search volume climbs for months, and short-form video is where the attention lands first. Most people start posting the week of launch. By then they are late. The channels that quietly built a back catalog and an audience beforehand collect the launch-week spike.

The bottleneck was never ideas. It was turning one trailer into a stack of finished, on-format clips fast enough to post daily without burning out. That is a pipeline problem. So I built the pipeline and open-sourced it.

1What it does

One command reads a topic and a trailer, and writes a complete content batch to disk:

  • A script card per Short — hook, five script beats, subtitle style, thumbnail brief, title, hashtags.
  • A master prompt you paste into Claude to get the voiceover scripts.
  • A posting calendar (CSV) — one clip per day through the window.
  • A machine-readable plan (JSON) so the rest of your tools can pick it up.

Every hook runs through a quality gate that flags weak or slop-sounding openers before you spend a minute shooting.

2The setup

1
Get the engine. It is a plain Node package, no API key needed to generate the batch.
npx gta6-shorts --topic "GTA 6" --count 10 --out ./batch
2
Grab any trailer link. Each official trailer is roughly ten Shorts waiting to be clipped. Every new one they release is ten more free ideas.
3
Run it. You get a folder of script cards, a master prompt, and a calendar. Open the master prompt, paste it into Claude with your trailer link, and it writes the voiceovers.

3The full loop

The engine does the thinking part. The rest is plug-in tools you already know:

  • Scripts + hooks + thumbnails + calendar → the pipeline (this).
  • Voiceovers → Claude, from the master prompt (slop-filtered).
  • Video → a generator like Higgsfield or Kling.
  • Voice + sound → ElevenLabs. Assembly → Creatomate (9:16).
  • Post daily through the pre-launch window, following the calendar.

The reusable part is the point. Swap --topic and the same pipeline works for any hype moment, not just this one.

4Why it works

Nobody scrolls past a "you missed this detail" clip on a launch everyone is already watching. The format triggers curiosity and the algorithm rewards watch time. The pre-launch window is the highest-leverage stretch: an audience built before the game drops means your first launch-day upload lands on people immediately, and the algorithm pushes it wider.

The honest part

No income promises here. This is a commodity content play in a dated window. There is no moat, it gets crowded, and the moment the window closes the edge is gone. What is real: gaming AdSense RPMs are public (roughly single-digit to low-double-digit dollars per thousand views), and a back catalog compounds. What you actually earn depends on your views, your niche, and your consistency, which no tool can promise.

Use official trailers under fair-use / commentary, respect each platform's terms and copyright, and note this is a fan tool, not affiliated with Rockstar. The pipeline is the value. The judgement is yours.

5Start today

The window is open now. Every day you wait is a day of catalog your future audience is handing to someone faster. Run the command, generate a batch, post the first clip.
